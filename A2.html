Summary

In this exploratory data analysis, we delve into the relationship that single-family house zoning has with geography, demography, income and household types within Massachusetts. We find that while there are no strong spatial patterns in which these zones are located, they are highly correlated with income (more high-income earners and less low-income earners reside in SFHZ areas), demography (non-white residents are less likely to reside in SFHZ) and household types (more family households in SFHZ areas than non-family households). However, there is more work to be done to disentangle the discovered relationships, and it is unclear if such patterns are a result of SFHZ, or if the underlying factors caused more SFHZ to arise in the areas.
